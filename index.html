SoC & SoD

  1. Overview
    MDG Space brings to you **Season of Code and Design (SoC&D)**, our annual month-long mentorship program where you work on solving real world problems and go from pitch to product under the guidance of the best in the business. You’ll learn by *doing*: as you develop and design, you’ll discover and explore and make and break new and exciting tech! For many participants, this program jump-starts their journey as a developer or designer, giving them an edge over others. All you need is the enthusiasm to build something. We’re here to help with everything else.
  2. Who?
    • SoC&D’23 is open to UG 1st and 2nd year students of IIT Roorkee
    • There are two tracks: Development and Design
    • In the Development track, both solo and team participation is allowed. A maximum of 3 developers can team up for this track.
    • In the Design track, only **solo participation** is allowed.
    • You can apply to both tracks at the same time.
  3. What?
    You can build absolutely anything! There will be no restrictions on the tech stack used in the project. You can make apps, host a website, write bots, games, plugins, libraries or even extensions.
  4. How?
    • You need an idea. Over the next few days, we’ll be gradually releasing around 10 problem statements. These problem statements can be found at the bottom of this page and also on in the #socd-23 channel of MDG Space Open Slack. You can think of ideas that can solve those problem statements, but don’t let that restrict your creativity. If you have an idea that’s not a listed problem statement, we’d love to hear it too!
    • Check your eligibility and choose a track to participate in. If you’re participating in the Development track, decide whether you want to team up or ride solo.
    • Choose an appropriate template from the ones listed below and get to work filling it with your project’s details. Research your idea, come up with features, milestones and deliverables. If at any point you get stuck, Google is your best friend!
    • If you are ready with a draft proposal and want to get it reviewed before you submit, you can DM it to any person with a “.mdg” in their username on our Slack workspace.
    • Keep an eye out on our social media handles for the submission links.
